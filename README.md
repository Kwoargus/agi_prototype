# agi_prototype

AGI should be structurally similar to human intelligence and functionally equivalent to it. 
Current architectures (Transformers, MoE) demonstrate outstanding results in narrow tasks, 
but do not reproduce the hierarchical organization of cognitive mechanisms, which we believe 
is a necessary condition for achieving general intelligence.

The core idea of our approach is that human intelligence itself has its own multi-level 
structure (not to be confused with the structure of the brain). Each level of perception 
is responsible for processing external signals of a specific type, from the simplest signals 
such as sound, smell, touch, retinal images, etc., to complex signals such as speech, 
scientific theories, works of art, etc. 
We assume that a neural network analogue of human intelligence will be able to approach the 
functionality of human intelligence if it replicates the structure of human intelligence. 
Only then a neural network analogue of human intelligence will be able to perform intellectual 
work in the same way as human intelligence. To realize this, we need to formulate the structure 
of human intelligence (not the brain, but the cognitive apparatus itself). This structure 
proposed and described in article [https://mtnip.elpub.ru/jour/article/view/35] In this article 
also proposed an algorithm for training a neural network analogue of human intelligence at each 
level and the entire predictor using tools consisting of generative networks and genetic 
algorithms.


The Essence of the Approach (5-Level Architecture)
We identify five evolutionarily conditioned components of the human psyche, each of which should 
be reflected in the AGI architecture:

Level "Reflexes"
    Function: Instantaneous response to threshold stimuli
    Technical Interpretation: Fast anomaly detectors / safety triggers
Level "Instincts"
    Function: Pattern-oriented behavior
    -Technical Interpretation: Library of learned scenarios + prediction of consequences
Level "Emotions"
    Function: Social synchronization and value assessment
    Technical Interpretation: Module for assessing the "usefulness" of actions for the group
Level "Intelligence"
    Function: Creation and use of dynamic predictive models
    Technical Interpretation: Generative model engine + "intuition + analogy" mechanism
Level "Consciousness"
    Function: Meta-model "Self-Group-World"
    Technical Interpretation: Context manager supporting behavioral integrity


Key Mechanisms

Knowledge Graph: Storage of knowledges (Nodes) and relationships (Edges) allows to find related 
and similar knowledges.

Model Storage: Objects and processes are encoded as sequences of states.

Generating new knowledge: an "intuition + analogy" mechanism—trying, modifying, and validating 
existing models to describe new situations.

Learning: a combination of generative networks and genetic algorithms, where selection occurs 
based on the criterion "promotes the survival and development of life"—which provides a built-in 
ethical orientation.
